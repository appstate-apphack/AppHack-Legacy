Seminars


At AppHack, speakers across a variety of disciplines share their knowledge by giving 20 minute seminars. Thank you to our teachers!

Aleksander Ratzloff :: Game Programming With SDL



Alek, a CS Undergraduate at Appalachian State University, gives pro tips on effectively developing a game using the SDL graphical library. Alek gathers his knowledge from years of experience in 2D game development and the C++ programming language.

Christopher Hegre :: Software Deployment Strategies



Chris, a CS undergraduate at Appalachian State University, discusses ways to get a working application out quickly and safely while maximizing your user base. Chris is the creator/founder of several widely-used products such as Darkland Servers, AppalApp, and Beepdrive.

Dr. Jay Fenwick :: The Collective Coding Movement



Dr. Fenwick kicks off AppHack 0 with an abridged history lesson on the collective coding movement and software product workflow. Dr. Fenwick shares his expertise from years of teaching mobile application development and past work in the airplane manufacturing industry.

Nathan Hernandez :: Gadgetz



Nate, a CS undergraduate at Appalachian State University, gives an informative lesson on lower-level programming with the Arduino microcontroller. Nate, a co-founder of the well-known mobile application Beepdrive, has a strong background in embedded systems programming and lower-level architecture.

Dr. Rahman Tashakkori :: Digital Image Processing



Dr. Tashakkori, a CS professor at Appalachian State University, explains theory and practice of modern digital image processing techniques. Dr. Tashakkori shares his knowledge from years of research in medical imaging, edge detection, and other forms of visualization in the physical sciences.

Shay Cichocki :: Crash Into Flask



Shay, a Computer Science alumnus from Appalachian State University, gives a crash course on creating a dynamic and robust web application using the Python-based Flask framework. Shay, a fervid programmer since the age of 16, has a passion for teaching young and aspiring software developers.
