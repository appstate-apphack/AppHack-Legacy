Rules


  1. No weapons or alcohol.
  2. Be respectful of other attendees.
  3. All coding and the majority of asset work must be done during the event (April 5th 6pm - April 6th 12pm).
    1. UMLs, concept art, and to-do lists are allowed beforehand.
  4. At least one team member must be present for the demo fair. Prizes will not be awarded to teams that are not present.
  5. All teams retain full ownership of what they create during AppHack.
  6. Category-specific rules:
    1. To win the Outstanding Freshman prize, everyone on the team must either be in CS 2440 (or equivalent) currently, OR have not yet taken it.
    2. To win the Women in CS prize, at least half the team members must identify as women.
    3. To win the Best Algorithm Design prize, the team must submit a written explanation of their algorithm. Click here for guidelines and an example for this writeup.

Workshops


At AppHack, speakers across a variety of disciplines share their knowledge by giving seminars. Thank you to all who have agreed to spread their knowledge! You can find the workshop schedule here!

Scott Bradley :: Guessing your future and the soft skills that will make you successful



Directed conversation around industry trends that will affect what/how/where you work in the coming years. We will combine these trends with learning opportunities, and the associates soft skills that can distinguish you as an employee.

Gurney Buchanan :: How fast can you add a billion numbers?



How fast can you add a billion numbers? In this program, we explore parallelization by asking and answering that simple question. We’ll explore CPU and GPU parallelization in an introductory look at massively parallelizable problems and solutions.

Adam Dixon :: From Monolith to Microservice: Service Based Architecture with Queues



As business requirements continue to grow, and feature sets continue to expand, the typical single application is no longer sustainable as hardware requirements increase. Due to this, the monolithic application must be broken up into services that can each scale to support dynamic loads.

Evan Jones :: Build and Deploy Your First Website



Learn the basics of HTML and CSS to make a beautiful personal website. Then, deploy your website so that others can find you and your work!

Jordan Meyer :: Structured Experimentation - Practical Lessons from Winning The Zillow Prize


The $1M Zillow Prize competition brought together nearly 4,000 teams from 91 countries to compete against the Zestimate, and one another, to most accurately predict the sale prices of homes across the U.S. Because machine-learning competitions have a reputation for rewarding overly-complex ensembles, Zillow Prize required submissions to be reproducible, with limits on hardware and processing time.

Keith Pohl :: Turning User Stories Into Products



Not everyone speaks code. We will be sharing how we interface between customers and our development team to bring products to life.

Andrew Thorp :: The joy of concurrency in Go



Setting up concurrency can be a nightmare in many languages. Go offers a concurrency paradigm that allows you to engineer your programs around concurrent thread safe communication. This workshop will give an introduction to concurrency in go; concepts, syntax, semantics, and how to use it effectively.

Mike Wilson :: Kubernetes 101



What is Kubernetes and what can it do?
