Seminars


At AppHack, speakers across a variety of disciplines share their knowledge by giving 20 minute seminars. Thank you to our teachers!

Brynne Elozory :: The Design Process



Brynne, a designer at Appalachian State University, gives a walkthrough on how to properly design an eye-catching product from start to finish. Brynne is the current president of the Student Art League and is passionate about promoting the work of young artists and designers like herself.

Kevin Chabreck :: Easy Socket Programming in Python



Kevin is a CS undergrad at Appalachian State University and soon to be PhD student at Duke University. He has demonstrated prowess in the networking field with the development of several applications, including a multiuser web-based chat room and a web-based collaborative paint program.

Alex Hudson :: Technical Internship Secrets



Alex is a CS undergrad at Appalachian State University and an avid Google Glass developer. A recent series of summer internship interviews inspired Alex to share his knowledge on how to be well prepared for technical interviews and how to excel once acquiring an internship position.

Aaron Brewbaker :: CUDA Programming in F#



Aaron is a CS Masters student at Appalachian State University. He is an intern for Quant Alea, a swiss based company specializing in GPU programming. He has aided Quant Alea in building a software solution to develop CUDA accelerated GPU applications on the .NET framework.

Eric Everson :: Understanding Organizational Culture



Eric is a CS undergrad at Appalachian State University and a fervid web developer. As the treasure for the Association of Computing Machinery chapter at Appalachian State University, Eric shares how factors such as product, market, and technology influence the principles of an organization.

Jon Johnson :: The Vim Text Editor 101



Jon is a CS undergrad at Appalachian State University, a past intern at IBM jStart, and a current intern at PassportParking. Jon specializes in mobile-application development and server-side web development. He has also been using the VIM text editor as long as he can remember and shares his proficiency by teaching the basics of how to use the tool.
