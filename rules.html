AppHack Rules


In order to be a prize winner of any AppHack you must abide by the following set of rules:
1. The majority of coding and actual asset work must be started on the night of AppHack and must cease 18 hours after AppHack has commenced. UMLs, concept art, to-do lists all allowed beforehand.

2. You or a teammate must be present upon project submission to recieve a prize. Prizes will not be awarded to teams that are not present.

3. Winning entries will be decided by a voting panel consisting of professors, AppHack sponsors, and CS Graduate Students.

4. Your product must not be something that you previously created whether it be in or out of class.

5. Your product must consist of your own original code and noone else's. Code will be checked for plagiarism. If you use a library, API, framework or opensource project you must reference it in your code and not take credit for it.

6. Your product must be at some point of completion upon judging. You will not get credit for what you do not deliver.

7. If you worked on the product with other parties, those parties will recieve an evenly split portion of the prize unless otherwise decided on by all members of the group

8. All teams retain full ownership of what they create during AppHack.

9. You must be respectful and accepting to all individuals present at AppHack.
