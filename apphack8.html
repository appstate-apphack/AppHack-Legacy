Seminars


At AppHack, speakers across a variety of disciplines share their knowledge by giving 30 minute seminars. Thank you to all who have agreed to spread their knowledge!

Dr. Raghuveer Mohan :: Binary Trees



Dr. Raghu will discuss an algorithm technique design that allows computer scientists to solve problems using a binary searching theory. This theory works by "guessing" and having a binary search tell the user if the answer is too high or too low. Dr. Mohan will present several interesting problems that use the technique.

Jesse Cochran :: Android Mobile Development



Jesse, an undergraduate in Computer Science here at App State, will be presenting an introduction into the Android platform, the most widely used mobile operating system in the world. He will also be discussing using Android for Virtual Reality applications, apps for Android Wearables, and more!

Narayana Walters :: Making Games as a Hobby



Narayana, a senior in Comp Sci here at App and experienced traveller, is a hobbyist game developer, and has released his own game on Steam. In his seminar, he will overview how he began making games, and how others can start their own path into creating games.

Christopher Waldon :: Using Linux: Merits of an Alternative Operating System



Representing his club, Linux Users Club, Chris is incredibly passionate about the power of Linux and other open-source software to improve lives. In his presentation, he will discuss the benefits of Linux, tips and tricks to using it, and the unique features that Linux has compared to other operating systems.

Video Game Development Club :: Game Development - an Overview



The officers of VGDC will present a workshop to help people acquaint themselves with the basics of Unity, in order to create a simple game. Additionally, they will discuss an overview to planning the creation of a game.
